Vision

Provincial Library Balochistan aims to provide quality materials and services which fulfill educational, informational, cultural, and recreational needs of the entire community in an atmosphere that is welcoming and respectful. To be the welcoming heart of our community where all come to learn, discover, create, connect and succeed. The library fulfills this mission in an enthusiastic and professional manner while maintaining the respect and dignity of its staff and partons with excellence and superior quality of service.

History

United States Information Service (USIS) gave library services up to 1970 and
closed these services due to some priorities and handed over to Shaheed
Hakeem Muhammad Saeed who was the Chairman of the Society for
Promotion and Improvement of Libraries (SPIL) with one year’s budget.
He further gave it to the Commissioner Quetta Division and it was named as
“Quetta Divisional Library”. During Mengal regime Mr. Gul Khan Naseer the
then Education Minister renamed it as “Balochistan Public Library” in 1972.
During 1982 a Technical Working Group was formed for the inspection of
public libraries of country. The group inspected the Sandman Public Library for
two days and this library for three days and recommended this library to be
declared as Provincial Library. So in 1983 it was declared as Provincial Library
Balochistan.
